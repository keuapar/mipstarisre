Why am I doing this?

How many complicated polynomials have you tried to solve? It gets difficult very quickly.

In fact, the difficulty of deciding whether an equation like that on screen is true or false scales exponentially with the highest polynomial degree and also scales exponentially with the number of variables involved.

This makes it an exceptionally difficult problem for computers to solve efficiently, unless we allow for a specific margin of error.

Imagine reformulating the equation like this: $$(x+y)(x-y) - (x^2 - y^2) = 0$$

Then, you can just evaluate it at more and more random points: $$f(x=5, y=9) = 0 ?$$ The more =0 answers you get, the higher your confidence that the polynomials are indeed equal!
